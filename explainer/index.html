How to Write
Your Name in Korean

한글 [Hangeul] : the Korean alphabetic system, introduced in the 15th century, containing 14 consonants and 11 vowels.

1. Divide your name into syllable by pronunciation.
-Do not divide them by the spelling!
-You are writing what you are listening
2. From the table, find korean alphabets that have the same sound per syllable. You can find the sound that is closest to your name. Write each syllable in Korean language.
3. Combine the consonant and vowels per syllable.
-standing vowels always stand to the right of the consonant
-laying vowels always lay below the consonant
4. Great Job! All Done
Thank You!
